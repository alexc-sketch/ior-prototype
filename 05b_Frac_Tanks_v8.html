Container Frac Tanks

Container Frac Tanks (Skidded)

High-capacity containerised storage for fluid management on drilling programs. Available in ISO containerised or three-beam skid configurations, with an 8" external manifold for linking multiple tanks to create a high-capacity storage farm.

  • Capacity: 450bbl or 500bbl nominal
  • Dimensions: 40’L × 8’W × 8’6”H or 45’L × 8’W × 8’6”H
  • Construction: ¼” Q235 carbon steel, smooth wall
  • Floor: V-bottom on rectangular tubing with forklift pockets
  • Manifold: 8” external manifold for tank linking
  • Inlet/Outlet: 4” inlet (front & rear), 4” outlets with ANSI 150 flanges & butterfly valves, 4” bottom suction at rear
  • Liner: Carboline epoxy resin
  • Welds: All seams double welded, all welds continuous
  • Config: ISO containerised or 3-beam skid
  • Options: Pumps, solar power units, agitators

Mobile Frac Tanks

Mobile Frac Tanks — 500bbl (Square)

Designed for rapid deployment and recovery across remote drilling programs. Fitted with a 2” ADR kingpin and Ringfeeder for roadtrain combinations, and diesel-powered hydraulic lifting cylinders for effortless loading onto a prime mover.

  • Capacity: 500bbl / 80,000L
  • Dimensions: 40’L × 8’W × 11’H (overall nominal)
  • Transport: 2” ADR kingpin & Ringfeeder for roadtrain combination
  • Loading: Diesel-powered hydraulic lifting cylinders
  • Access: Ladder & platform at rear for top roof hatch inspection
  • Gel line: 4” front connection (recirculation) with ANSI flange & butterfly valve, internal pipe to rear
  • Inlet/Outlet: Two 4” direct inlet/outlet at rear; 4” bottom suction at side rear; 8” manifold with 4 × 4” suction connections
  • Level: Sight gauge level indicators at side
  • Welds: All seams double welded both sides, continuous

Open Top DST Tank — 400bbl

Open Top DST Tanks

Open Top DST Tanks — 180bbl & 400bbl

Purpose-built for drill stem testing (DST) and produced water management. Features a top viewing platform with fold-up handrail for safe fluid inspection, and a 2” internal jet line for fluid agitation and recirculation.

  • Capacity: 180bbl or 400bbl nominal
  • Dimensions: 20’L × 8’W × 8’6”H (180bbl) or 40’L × 8’W × 8’6”H (400bbl)
  • Construction: ¼” Q235 carbon steel, smooth wall
  • Floor: V-bottom on rectangular tubing with forklift pockets
  • Manifold: 8” external manifold with 4” outlets, ANSI 150 flanges & butterfly valves
  • Agitation: 2” internal jet line for fluid recirculation
  • Access: Top viewing platform with fold-up handrail & fold-down stairway
  • Lifting: Certified lifting points at top
  • Liner: Carboline epoxy resin
  • Config: Double-ended 3-beam skid or ISO containerised
  • Options: 4” trash pumps, transfer hose, hose baskets

Frequently asked questions

Our frac tank range spans from 180bbl (approximately 28,600L) up to 500bbl (approximately 80,000L). The Open Top DST Tanks are available in 180bbl and 400bbl capacities, while the Container Frac Tanks and Mobile Frac Tanks are available in 450bbl and 500bbl configurations respectively.

Yes. Our Container Frac Tanks feature an 8" external manifold specifically designed for linking multiple tanks together. This allows you to create a scalable, high-capacity storage farm on site — ideal for large drilling programs or extended fluid management requirements. Each tank also features 4" outlets with ANSI 150 flanges and butterfly valves for controlled inter-tank transfer.

The 500bbl Mobile Frac Tanks are fitted with a 2" ADR kingpin and Ringfeeder, making them fully compatible with roadtrain combinations for long-haul transport to remote sites. They also feature diesel-powered hydraulic lifting cylinders, which allow the tank to be loaded and unloaded from a prime mover without the need for additional lifting equipment on site.

Yes. IOR has a dedicated logistics fleet with extensive experience delivering and installing infrastructure to the most remote project locations across Australia. Whether your site is in the Pilbara, the Cooper Basin, or the Northern Territory, our team has the capability and experience to get your tanks on site and operational.

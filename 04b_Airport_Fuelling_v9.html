How It Works

From terminal to wingtip: a JIG-compliant process.

IOR's quality control process is built around the JIG standard. Every fuelling event is documented, every filter is checked, and every delivery is traceable.

1
Fuel receipt & terminal testing
Batch certificates verified against Jet A-1 and Avgas specifications.
2
Pre-departure checks
Filter DP, water tests, and equipment serviceability confirmed daily.
3
Aircraft confirmation
Registration, fuel grade, and authorised quantity confirmed with the crew.
4
Into-plane fuelling
JIG-compliant bonding, filter checks, and visual quality checks during delivery.
5
Digital records
Complete fuelling records automatically uploaded to the Customer Portal.

Frequently Asked Questions

Airport fuelling — your questions answered.

JIG (Joint Inspection Group) is the international standard for aviation fuel quality and handling. JIG-compliant into-plane fuelling means that every fuelling event is conducted by trained operators following strict procedures. This includes pre-departure checks, aircraft identification, and water checks during delivery.

IOR supplies Jet A-1 for commercial and turbine aircraft, and Avgas 100LL for piston-engine aircraft. UL91 is also available at selected locations. Batch certificates are available on request.

Yes. IOR operates a dedicated aviation fleet. Our refuellers and hydrant dispensers are used exclusively for aviation fuels and are never used for ground fuels. This eliminates the risk of switch loading contamination.

IOR operates at contracted airports across Australia, including major regional airports and remote airstrips. Visit our Aviation Network page for a full list of current locations.

Every IOR fuelling event generates a complete digital record including aircraft registration, fuel grade, volume dispensed, operator ID, and timestamp. Records are available via the IOR Customer Portal. Safety Data Sheets are available on request.

We understand that these operations have no margin for supply failure, and our sovereign supply chain and dedicated aviation fleet are designed to support mission-critical operations.

The Challenge

Australia's fuel supply chain is more fragile than most operators realise.

Global Supply Disruption

Middle East tensions, shipping delays, and refinery outages can cut off supply with little warning. Operators who rely on a single supplier are exposed.

Insufficient Local Storage

Australia holds fewer days of fuel reserve than most developed nations. Without strategic local storage, a supply shock becomes an operational shutdown.

Sovereign Risk

Imported fuel is subject to geopolitical risk. Operators in critical industries — mining, defence, agriculture — need a supply partner with genuine sovereign credentials.

Import & Storage

Deep-water import terminals

IOR operates two of Australia's most strategic diesel import and storage terminals, providing 191 million litres of combined capacity. Our Lytton (QLD) and Port Bonython (SA) facilities ensure absolute fuel security for Australian industry.

  • 191ML combined diesel storage capacity
  • 2,400 Lpm high-speed loading gantries
  • Triple road train access, open 24/7

Domestic Refinery

Australia's inland oil refinery

Operating since 1984, our Eromanga refinery processes locally produced crude oil to produce Eromanga UMF — the lowest-particulate diesel mining fuel on the market. It stands as a critical asset for Australia's sovereign fuel security.

  • 1,250 barrels per day refining capacity
  • Produces ultra-low particulate Eromanga UMF
  • Sulphur content <10ppm — exceeds Australian standards
  • 40+ years of continuous outback operation

Supply & Trading — common questions

IOR's Supply & Trading division manages the import, storage, refining, and wholesale distribution of diesel and petroleum products across Australia. This includes operating two deep-water import terminals at Lytton (QLD) and Port Bonython (SA), the Eromanga Refinery in outback Queensland, and trading condensate products as part of our broader supply chain operations.

In April 2026, IOR entered a commercial framework with the Federal Government and Export Finance Australia, making IOR Australia's designated sovereign fuel supply partner. This government-backed mandate recognises IOR's unique position as the only independent fuel company with its own import terminals and domestic refinery, providing genuine supply security for critical Australian industries.

Our Story

IOR is fuelling Australia.
This is our story.

IOR is a family-owned, integrated supplier of fuels to regional, rural, and remote Australia. From humble beginnings in 1984 as a small oil refinery in the far western Queensland town of Eromanga, we've built a deep connection to the heartbeat of regional Australia — from the bush to the beach.

Our approach is built on community and the integrity to do the right thing. We're determined to push the bar, innovate, and continue to help, develop, and grow Australian communities.

Our History

40 years of building
Australia's fuel backbone.

1984
IOR founded in Queensland
Founded as a fuel distributor serving Queensland's transport and agricultural sectors.
1990s
Expansion into aviation fuels
IOR enters the aviation fuel market, establishing relationships with regional airports and agricultural aviation operators across Queensland.
2005
Lytton Terminal acquisition
IOR acquires the Lytton Terminal in Brisbane, securing direct import capability and removing reliance on third-party supply chains.
2012
HyDip™ technology launched
IOR develops and launches HyDip™ — Australia's first integrated fuel management, dispensing, and payment platform built for commercial operators.
2018
Eromanga Refinery acquired
IOR acquires the Eromanga Refinery in outback Queensland — Australia's only privately owned inland oil refinery — cementing sovereign supply capability.
2021
Port Bonython Terminal
IOR expands its terminal infrastructure with the Port Bonython Terminal in South Australia, strengthening national supply chain resilience.
2024
Stonepeak partnership
Strategic infrastructure capital partnership with Stonepeak, backing IOR's national expansion and next-generation digital platform development.

We own the infrastructure.
We won't run out.

IOR is not a reseller. We own Lytton Terminal, Port Bonython Terminal, and the Eromanga Refinery — Australia's only privately owned inland oil refinery. Our owned infrastructure provides a buffer against global supply chain volatility. That's what sovereign supply actually means.

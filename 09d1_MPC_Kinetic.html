The Challenge

The Tanami Natural Gas Pipeline project required fuel supply to five remote locations across one of Australia's most isolated and logistically demanding environments — the Tanami Desert in the Northern Territory. MPC Kinetic needed a fuel partner who could supply and install bulk diesel storage infrastructure, manage ongoing top-up deliveries, and provide 24/7 operational support across sites with no permanent road access and extreme weather conditions.

The project demanded not just fuel supply, but a complete infrastructure and logistics solution — with satellite connectivity for remote monitoring and strict security and induction compliance requirements for all personnel accessing the sites.

The Solution

IOR supplied nine self-bunded bulk diesel storage tanks ranging from 10KL to 100KL across five key locations, including installation and commissioning. Each tank was equipped with HyDip™ remote monitoring technology, allowing IOR's operations team to track fuel levels and usage via satellite from Brisbane — enabling proactive top-up scheduling and eliminating the risk of unplanned fuel outages at remote sites.

  • Nine self-bunded tanks supplied, installed, and commissioned across five locations
  • Top-up deliveries coordinated from Port Bonython Terminal via road train
  • HyDip™ satellite-connected remote monitoring on all tanks
  • 24/7 on-call maintenance and emergency response capability
  • Full security induction compliance for all IOR personnel accessing project sites
  • Community investment: IOR supported the Red Dust Role Models Break the Cycle 2018 program in the Tanami region

IOR drew on experience and learnings from its national diesel network — which reaches some of the most remote corners of Australia — to deliver a solution that met the project's demanding timeline and operational requirements.

"IOR demonstrated its ability to deliver in the most remote and demanding environments in Australia. Their experience, their technology, and their commitment to the project made the difference."
— Brendan McGuckin, MPC Kinetic

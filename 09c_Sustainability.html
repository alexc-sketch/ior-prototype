Health & Safety

Everyone goes home safely. Every day.

IOR is committed to providing a workplace that is safe for both team members and customers. All team members at IOR are responsible for ensuring health and safety is managed throughout the company.

Through a consultative approach, IOR continues to develop and improve safe operating procedures that are practical. Team members receive regular training to help them champion identifying and minimising risks in their day-to-day duties at IOR.

IOR holds third party certification through independent registration body QAS International for ISO 45001:2018 & AS/NZS 4801:2001 – Occupational Health and Safety Management Systems.

Environmental Management

Environmental stewardship in every region we serve.

IOR holds third party certification from QAS International for ISO 14001:2015 – Environmental Management. Under this certification, IOR seeks to minimise the risk of adversely impacting the environment.

This is achieved by consulting and working collaboratively with the communities in which the company operates.

IOR team members seek to understand the environmental resources that these communities rely upon so that reasonable steps can be taken to provide high-quality products and services responsibly.

In the remote and regional environments where IOR operates, environmental stewardship is not optional — it is a licence to operate.

IOR HSE Management System

A fully integrated approach to safety, environment, and quality.

IOR's management system is certified to PAS 99:2012 — Integrated Management Systems — covering ISO 9001:2015 (Quality), ISO 14001:2015 (Environment), ISO 45001:2018 (OHS), and AS/NZS 4801:2001.

This integrated approach ensures that safety, quality, and environmental performance are managed as a unified system — not in silos.

IOR's health and safety framework is built on the principle that every team member goes home safely — every day, without exception.

Our safety management system is certified to ISO 45001:2018 and AS/NZS 4801:2001, covering terminal operations, bulk fuel delivery, remote site infrastructure, and aviation fuelling.

  • Mandatory safety inductions for all site personnel and contractors
  • Pre-start safety checks on all delivery vehicles and equipment
  • Incident reporting and root cause analysis for all near-misses and incidents
  • Regular safety audits across all terminals, sites, and network sites
  • Dedicated HSEQ team led by GM People, Culture & Safety
  • Drug and alcohol testing program across all operational roles
  • Emergency response plans for all terminal and refinery operations
  • Consultative approach to developing and improving safe operating procedures

IOR operates in environments where environmental stewardship is not optional — it is a licence to operate.

Our environmental management approach is certified to ISO 14001:2015 and covers spill prevention, contamination management, waste reduction, and responsible infrastructure design.

  • Self-bunded tank design on all IOR-supplied storage infrastructure — zero ground contamination risk
  • Spill containment systems at all terminal and site locations
  • Environmental impact assessments for all new infrastructure deployments
  • Responsible disposal of all fuel-contaminated materials and equipment
  • Ongoing monitoring of environmental performance at Eromanga Refinery
  • Collaborative approach with communities to understand and protect local environmental resources
  • Commitment to reducing Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions across operations

IOR's quality management system is certified to ISO 9001:2015, ensuring consistent delivery of high-quality fuel products and services across every customer touchpoint.

Quality is not just a product specification — it is a commitment to every customer, every delivery, every time.

  • ISO 9001:2015 certification across terminal and distribution operations
  • Fuel quality testing at Lytton and Port Bonython terminals
  • Traceability of all fuel products from terminal to customer
  • Customer complaint management and resolution process
  • Continuous improvement program across all operational processes
  • Supplier quality assessment for all fuel and equipment procurement

IOR's governance framework reflects the responsibilities that come with operating critical national fuel infrastructure.

As a Stonepeak-backed business, IOR operates to institutional governance standards — with board oversight, independent audit, and transparent reporting across all material risk areas.

  • Board-level oversight of all material risk and compliance matters
  • Independent external audit of financial and operational performance
  • Anti-bribery and corruption policy covering all employees and contractors
  • Modern slavery statement published annually
  • Data privacy and cybersecurity framework aligned with Australian Privacy Act
  • Whistleblower policy with independent reporting channel
  • Regulatory compliance across all state and federal environmental and safety obligations

IOR is committed to transparent ESG reporting as part of its obligations to customers, partners, employees, and the communities it serves. Our ESG framework covers environmental performance, social impact, and governance standards across the entire IOR Group.

  • Annual ESG report covering all material sustainability metrics
  • Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions reporting for all operational facilities
  • Community investment reporting — financial and in-kind contributions
  • Workforce diversity and inclusion metrics
  • Supply chain sustainability assessment for all major suppliers
  • TCFD-aligned climate risk disclosure (in progress)

Our Commitments

How IOR approaches safety,
environment, and governance.

IOR's health and safety framework is built on the principle that every team member goes home safely — every day, without exception. Our safety management system is aligned with ISO 45001 and covers every operational environment, from terminal operations and bulk fuel delivery to remote site infrastructure and aviation fuelling.

  • Mandatory safety inductions for all site personnel and contractors
  • Pre-start safety checks on all delivery vehicles and equipment
  • Incident reporting and root cause analysis for all near-misses and incidents
  • Regular safety audits across all terminals, depots, and network sites
  • Dedicated HSEQ team led by GM People, Culture & Safety
  • Drug and alcohol testing program across all operational roles
  • Emergency response plans for all terminal and refinery operations

IOR operates in environments where environmental stewardship is not optional — it is a licence to operate. Our environmental management approach is aligned with ISO 14001 and covers spill prevention, contamination management, waste reduction, and responsible infrastructure design.

  • Self-bunded tank design on all IOR-supplied storage infrastructure — zero ground contamination risk
  • Spill containment systems at all terminal and depot locations
  • Environmental impact assessments for all new infrastructure deployments
  • Responsible disposal of all fuel-contaminated materials and equipment
  • Ongoing monitoring of environmental performance at Eromanga Refinery
  • Commitment to reducing Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions across operations

IOR's governance framework reflects the responsibilities that come with operating critical national fuel infrastructure. As a Stonepeak-backed business, IOR operates to institutional governance standards — with board oversight, independent audit, and transparent reporting across all material risk areas.

  • Board-level oversight of all material risk and compliance matters
  • Independent external audit of financial and operational performance
  • Anti-bribery and corruption policy covering all employees and contractors
  • Modern slavery statement published annually
  • Data privacy and cybersecurity framework aligned with Australian Privacy Act
  • Whistleblower policy with independent reporting channel

IOR is committed to transparent ESG reporting as part of its obligations to customers, partners, employees, and the communities it serves. Our ESG framework covers environmental performance, social impact, and governance standards across the entire IOR Group.

  • Annual ESG report published covering all material sustainability metrics
  • Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions reporting for all operational facilities
  • Community investment reporting — financial and in-kind contributions
  • Workforce diversity and inclusion metrics
  • Supply chain sustainability assessment for all major suppliers
  • TCFD-aligned climate risk disclosure (in progress)

IOR's social impact strategy is grounded in the communities where we operate. From Indigenous youth programs in remote Queensland to regional sponsorships and aeromedical partnerships, IOR invests in the people and places that make up the fabric of regional Australia.

  • Red Dust Role Models — Indigenous youth mentoring and health programs
  • LifeFlight aeromedical partnership — keeping emergency aviation services operational
  • AgForce partnership — supporting Queensland's rural industries
  • Regional sponsorships across sport, community events, and local organisations
  • IOR Apprenticeship Program — building the next generation of fuel industry professionals
  • School-based traineeships in regional Queensland communities
